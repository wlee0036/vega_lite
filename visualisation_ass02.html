Gender Differences in Suicide

Women are more likely to be diagnosed with suicidal thoughts,
but men died from suicide at a higher rate

In all countries, the rate of men suicides are greater than women suicides. Although women are well-known for higher rates of depression diagnoses as well as suicidal thoughts, men happen to die more from suicides.


Several factors contribute to this phenomena. One key element is the lack of communication. Society has, for many generations, encourage men to stay strong and not to cast out their emotions as it is believed as a sign of weakness. Looks like the 'boys don't cry' pressure is causing damaging mental health consequences.


Men suicides are highest in European countries. Gender disparity in suicide rate are also the biggest in this region. Meanwhile women suicides are highest in Asian countries. As can be seen, countries like Sri Lanka, Japan, Republic of Korea lead the rate of women suicide globally.

Since 1985 to 2016, there has been slightly decreasing trend in women suicide rate. On the other hand, men experienced a major spike during early 90s, reaching the peak at 1995 with approximately 1 out of every 4000 population committing suicide annually. The trend has then been decreasing steadily with better awareness and suicide prevention strategy by the government. Data for the unprecedented year 2020 would be interesting to look at to see if the trend continues given the tough time.
